How does the Venus Flytrap capture it's prey?

LINK 1

The Venus flytrap actually catches it's prey in an interesting way.

You may be wondering "How does the Venus flytrap catch prey without eyes?

The secret is actually inside of it's mouth.

It actually has little hairs inside of its mouth that act as a trigger when it is touched by an insect.

When one of these little hairs are touched by an insect, the venus flytrap instantly closes its mouth to trap the insect.

Then the venus flytrap oozes out its enzymes to digest the insect.

The best place to put a venus flytrap is probably near your fruit because fruit fries are attracted to it.
