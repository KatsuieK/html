The venus flytrap needs specific conditions for it to survive.

I am currently growing my own venus flytrap indoors.

Venus flytraps need a warm temperature to grow in and they also need peat moss instead of enriched soil.

They also produce asexually (meaning they do not need another organism of the same species to reproduce with).

The best way to plant venus flytrap seeds is to spread it out ontop of the soil instead of burying it inside of the soil.

It will take at least a week for the venus flytrap seeds to germinate and at least 4 - 5 weeks to grow into an adult flytrap.

The diet for the venus flytrap is usually 33% ants, 30% spiders, 10% beetles, 10% grasshoppers, and 5% flying insects.

Digestion takes about 10 days.
